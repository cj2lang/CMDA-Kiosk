For more than a century, LSU has cultivated a rich tradition of excellence in music, theater, dance, and film. We take great pride in the roles played by our faculty, staff, students, and alumni in preserving and growing the thriving arts communities of Louisiana, the region, the nation, and the world.

LSU’s earliest explorations of music and the arts date back to the military band traditions of the "Old War Skule" in the late 1800s. This history winds through a transformative century of development, expansion, global conflicts, and restructuring, evolving into the modern performing arts institution you see today, dedicated to the development of skillful learners who are adaptable creative artists and engaged global citizens.

School of Music

The Bachelor of Music degree prepares students for the world of musical performance. For this degree, we offer concentrations in:

  • Brass
  • Composition
  • Harp
  • Percussion
  • Piano
  • Piano Pedagogy
  • Strings
  • Voice
  • Woodwinds

Upon graduation, many students continue their studies at the master’s level, either at LSU or other prestigious institutions.

The Bachelor of Arts in Music degree prepares students for careers in arts administration, music business/industry, human services. For this degree, we offer concentrations in:

  • Academic Studies
  • Arts Administration
  • Church Music
  • Experimental Music & Digital Media (EMDM)
  • Intradisciplinary Music
  • Jazz
  • Theatre

Upon graduation, students have a wide variety of entrepreneurship opportunities and career paths available, or may pursue further study at the master’s level in music history or music theory.

The Bachelor of Music Education degree prepares students to be successful teachers in elementary and secondary schools, while continuing to hone their artistry and performance skills through participation in major performing ensembles.

This program’s curriculum is capped by the semester-long, site-based student teaching experience in which the student is guided and nurtured by a professional music teacher and a university coordinator. Teacher certification is granted by the Louisiana State Department of Education. Students graduate as confident and able professional music teachers and are highly competitive in securing teaching positions.

The Bachelor of Music Therapy prepares student musicians to become board-certified music therapists with student-centered guidance and an innovative academic curriculum. Students in the music therapy program will gain music knowledge and skills to then integrate into areas of clinical musicianship, clinical experience, and professional growth.

Students achieve this competence through rigorous and innovative academic programming in the areas of music, music therapy, research, behavioral and social science, and supervised music therapy clinical experiences in a variety of settings and populations of need

The Minor in Music allows students to deepen their musical artistry while simultaneously pursuing other academic studies. To graduate with a minor in music, students must complete approximately 17 credit-hours of music courses, electives, and applied lessons, with at least 3 of those hours being at the 3000 and/or 4000 level.

School of Theatre

Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) in Film & Television is designed to be a pre-professional degree program for students interested in entering the film and television industry upon graduation. This program allows students to explore various aspects of the production process including directing, screenwriting, and cinematography.

The Bachelor of Arts in Theatre degree provides students with a comprehensive view of theatre from a practical, theoretical, and historical viewpoint, while also providing them with direct production experiences and coursework tailored to their chosen area. For this degree, we offer concentrations in:

  • Arts Administration
  • Design & Technology
  • Performance
  • Physical Theatre
  • Theatre Studies

The Minor in Theatre allows students to develop their theatrical skills while simultaneously pursuing other academic studies. To graduate with a minor in theatre, students must select a desired concentration and then complete 18 credit-hours (Arts Administration, Dance, Physical Theatre) or 19 (Theatre) credit-hours of courses and electives

School of Music

The Master of Music degree is designed to continue students' fine arts studies with deeper focus and analysis. To allow for close alignment with a student’s desired sphere of focus, concentrations are offered in a wide variety of areas, such as performance, conducting, composition, collaborative piano, music theory, and more

The Doctor of Musical Arts degree is designed to prepare students to be artist-performers and conductors, in either university settings or the professional arena, with intensive coursework, seminars, and deep integration with a chosen area’s ongoing activities. This degree offers the following concentrations:

  • Performance (Brass, String, Piano, Percussion, Woodwind)
  • Coducting (Choral, Orchestral, Wind)
  • Collaborative Piano

The Doctor of Philosophy degree is designed to prepare students to be artistic scholars, researchers, or composers in both professional and university settings, with a strong focus on scholarship and pushing the boundaries of musical creative activity. This degree offers the following concentrations:

  • Composition
  • Experimental Music & Digital Media
  • Music Education
  • Musicology
  • Music Theory

School of Theatre

This program provides rigorous physical, vocal, emotional, and critical actor training approaches. The MFA acting students are the primary acting pool for the LSU Equity resident theatre company, Swine Palace. To promote holistic actors, candidates create and perform a 20- minute solo work of theatre as their thesis, concluding with an oral presentation on the creative process. A traditional written thesis option is also available.

This program provides a solid foundation of costume construction techniques, design, rendering, and management skills. Classroom instruction is complimented by hands-on experience in a variety of areas including draping, flat patterning, millinery, crafts, tailoring, etc. All students will undertake production positions on LSU Mainstage and Swine Palace productions.

Teaching, design and shop manager opportunities are available to for qualified students.

The LSU School of Theatre is proud to be one of the few departments in the country to offer a specialization in Properties Technology. The course of study includes a detailed examination of the many and varied items considered properties, and different techniques and materials that can be used to create them. The intention of this course of study is to develop advanced skills in woodworking, metalworking, paper-mâché, mold making & casting, foam carving, upholstery, sewing, and management. The curriculum is augmented with classes in scenic and costume design, painting for the theatre, CAD, and dramaturgy. All students will undertake production positions on LSU Mainstage and Swine Palace productions.

Teaching, design and shop manager opportunities are available to for qualified students.

This program prepares the theatre artisan/manager to undertake positions with a wide scope of responsibility. The intention of the course of study is to develop skills in scenic technology and design especially in the areas of structural analysis and design, entertainment rigging, theatre machinery and automation, production management, set and lighting design, and theatre safety. There is some flexibility in the program to better serve the student’s academic needs. All students will undertake production positions on LSU Mainstage and Swine Palace productions.

Teaching, design and shop manager opportunities are available to for qualified students.

The PhD in Theatre at LSU is a scholarly degree consisting of two or more years of in-person coursework in theatre and performance history, theory, criticism, and practice. This coursework then leads to the student proposing, researching, writing, and defending a substantial intervention into a critical subfield of theatre and performance studies (i.e., the dissertation.) We welcome dedicated researchers and practitioners who seek to complement their production skills with a rigorous engagement with theatre history, theory, and criticism.

We are committed to training leaders in the arts by adapting to meet the needs of the 21st-century artist. Our students are immersed in a curriculum promoting flexibility, discipline, communication, technology, and artistic expression, across all of our fields.

Undergraduate students who wish to enroll in our college must first apply to LSU, which uses the Common Application for first-year applicants. The Common App serves as the application for admission, as well as the application for all merit-based scholarships offered by LSU. This application is also used as your Ogden Honors College application, if you are interested. Scan the QR code to learn more.

If you are applying for a School of Music degree program, you will also need to complete an audition process in tandem with your application to LSU. To learn more, please visit the Music Auditions dropdown on this page.

 

Graduate students who wish to enroll in our college must first apply to the LSU Graduate School. The Graduate School evaluation entails a review of your resume or curriculum vita (CV), transcripts, TOEFL, IELTS, and PTE Scores (for international applicants), or degree certificates/diplomas (for international applicants). Following this step, our college determines your final admissibility to the degree program. Scan the QR code to learn more.

Your audition to enter the School of Music may be in-person or virtual, depending on your audition area. The requirements differ for each area as well, so be sure to check to see what applies to you. On-campus auditions are held during a few specific audition days each year. If you choose this option when filling out your application, you will be notified by our Office of Student Success of your audition date and time. Scan the QR code to learn more.

Get involved! We encourage all of our students to connect with the various clubs and professional organizations associated with our college. These include:

The Music & Dramatic Arts College Council serves as a liaison between Music & Dramatic Arts students, faculty, staff, and LSU Student Government. A core value and passion of the Council is improving student’s college experience. By raising money, putting on interactive events, and publicizing talents, the Council provides open lines of communication for student voices to be heard. Councils usually consist of five representatives, who are elected during the LSU Student Government’s spring and fall elections.

We are a student organization that focuses on encouraging participation for students interested in film and television within the School of Theatre. We are here to help students create content, connect with other film students, and foster a community of film.

Kappa Kappa Psi is an honorary service fraternity that serves the college or university band programs through service projects, fundraisers, social events, and other projects as needed. The fraternity is located at more than 150 colleges and universities. The Beta Gamma chapter of Kappa Kappa Psi was formed at LSU on May 16, 1948. In 1988, the chapter merged with LSU’s chapter of Tau Beta Sigma, the national honorary service sorority for college bands, and created a unified, co-ed organization. The Beta Gamma chapter serves the Department of Bands and the School of Music. Students interested in becoming members may submit applications to join at the beginning of each semester.

The mission of the Kids' Orchestra is to build a community of creative, confident, and socially engaged students through music education. We serve elementary-age children across East Baton Rouge Parish and provide them with the opportunity to study music, learn an instrument, and practice the art of performance. As an El Sistema-inspired organization, we have adopted the guiding philosophy that music education can create great musicians and positively change the life trajectory of children in a given community.

The Musical Theatre Club at LSU (MTC@LSU) provides opportunities to LSU's undergraduate and graduate students who have a passion for musical theatre. The organization's mission is to educate students in musical theatre. Additionally, MTC@LSU harnesses an appreciation for the genre of music theatre not only in the students, but in the Baton Rouge community as well. Club members can gain experience in all areas of musical theatre, from producing, to singing/dancing/acting, to playing in the orchestra, to technical production.

Sigma Alpha Iota (SAI), an international music fraternity for women, was organized in 1903 in Ann Arbor, Michigan. The organization and its members foster interests in music and promote social contact among persons sharing this interest. The Sigma Phi chapter was founded at LSU in 1926. The Sigma Phi chapter recruits new members during both the fall and spring semesters. LSU students interested in joining SAI must be enrolled as a full-time student, must have taken at least one music course, must have a minimum of a 2.5 grade-point average, and must have a great love of music. Students may not join during their first semesters but are encouraged to apply for admission during their following semesters.

The University Theatre Alliance is an organization of, by, and for the students of the School of Theatre. The executive board, along with its members and a team of coordinators, promotes a cohesive, supportive, effective, and convivial environment for creation and education. General membership meetings are held on a particular Wednesday of every month, typically announced on social media. Through social events, workshops, staged readings, and performance opportunities, organized by the student body, we become a stronger, smarter, and more experienced team of artists.

The Impact Fund of the College of Music & Dramatic Arts provides necessary educational and professional prospects to advance our performing arts students at LSU.

Our students need a broad array of opportunities to hear guest speakers, be able to extend their art to unique venues, travel to important conferences that address emerging trends in the performing arts, and engage in other activities that stretch their talents and ignite their curiosity. This will allow them to increase their capacity to negotiate collective creativity, elicit different goals and interests—in themselves and others-and participate in integrative problem solving.

There is also a need to provide collaborative opportunities among the members of our creative community—opportunities that go beyond the classroom and into other universities and the community at large. Experiences outside of the classroom, studio or stage provide our students with a well-rounded education that will equip them with the broad range of skills to be successful artist-citizens in the 21st century.

The Dean’s Circle is a group of alumni, parents, and friends of the College of Music & Dramatic Arts who support its students and faculty through annual, unrestricted, gifts.

These gifts are used at the discretion of the Dean of the college to support opportunities of student and faculty excellence, artistic endeavors, and annual programs. Membership provides a variety of benefits, most notably the guarantee that your philanthropy is enabling the development of artists and educators who will become the gold standard of the arts and arts education in this state, the nation, and the world.

We are in the final stages of planning an approximately $4.9 MM renovation to the School of Music building

The John G. Turner & Jerry G. Fischer Recital Hall will feature new seating, acoustical treatments, and a technical balcony for antiphonal work. The School of Music lobby, a vital nexus for our students and artists, will be expanded into a large open forum for collaboration and socialization.

This comprehensive re-envisioning of our most-used public spaces will completely transform everyday life in the College of Music & Dramatic Arts and give students the physical spaces they need to work together on projects and inspire new artistic works. This project reaffirms our commitment to being a premiere performing arts institution and cultural anchor for the city of Baton Rouge. These renovations are possible thanks to the generous contribution of donors, alumni, and friends of the LSU College of Music & Dramatic Arts.
